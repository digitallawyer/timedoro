üçÖ What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into focused intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

Why does it work?

  • Improved Focus: Short, focused sessions help maintain concentration
  • Reduced Mental Fatigue: Regular breaks prevent burnout
  • Better Time Awareness: Track how long tasks actually take
  • Increased Productivity: Urgency of time limit boosts efficiency

üöÄ How to Use Timedoro

Getting Started:

  1. Add Tasks: Use the task section to list what you want to work on
  2. Set Current Task: Click the circle (‚óã) next to a task to set it as current
  3. Start Timer: Click the "Start" button to begin your 25-minute focus session
  4. Take Breaks: When the timer ends, take the suggested break
  5. Repeat: Complete your configured number of sessions for a long break

Timer Controls:

  • Play/Pause (Spacebar): Start or pause the current session
  • Reset (R): Reset the current session to full time
  • Skip (S): Skip to the next session (focus/break)

‚ú® Features

Customization:

  • Timer Durations: Adjust focus time, short breaks, and long breaks
  • Session Count: Change how many sessions until a long break (2-8)
  • Themes: Choose from Light, Dark, Warm, or Cool themes
  • Ambient Sounds: Add background sounds to help you focus

Productivity Tools:

  • Task Management: Track your daily tasks and mark them complete. Create subtasks by pressing Tab while editing tasks
  • Session Notes: Add notes during each focus session
  • Progress Tracking: View daily and total session statistics
  • Achievements: Unlock badges for reaching productivity milestones
  • Streak Counter: Track consecutive days of using the technique

üí° Tips for Success

During Focus Sessions:

  • Close distracting apps and websites
  • Turn off non-essential notifications
  • Have a specific task or goal in mind
  • If you think of something else, write it down for later
  • Stay committed to the full 25 minutes

During Breaks:

  • Step away from your workspace
  • Do light physical activity (stretch, walk)
  • Avoid mentally demanding activities
  • Stay hydrated and have a healthy snack
  • Avoid checking emails or social media

Building the Habit:

  • Start with 2-3 pomodoros per day
  • Use the same workspace consistently
  • Review your progress weekly
  • Adjust timing based on what works for you
  • Celebrate your achievements!
